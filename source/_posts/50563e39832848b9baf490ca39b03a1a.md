This is one of the first Sri Lankan dishes that blew my mind and made me fall in love with the cuisine. It's a curry like none I had eaten previously. My favourite versions are the ones found at The Dutch Burgher Union, Gallery Cafe at Paradise Road and Barefoot in Colombo.
The almost coffee-like dark roasted spice powder, balanced with the fat from the pork belly and smoky citrus from the goraka, are a match made in heaven. It's crucial to ensure these three elements are in perfect harmony, a balance we have gone through hundreds of trials to achieve.

Serves 4

## Ingredients

- [ ] 500g (1lb 2oz) pork belly, skin-off and fat cap trimmed to no more than 1cm, cut into 5cm (2in) pieces
- [ ] 1½ tbsp chilli powder
- [ ] 1 tsp black pepper, coarsely ground
- [ ] 1½ tsp salt
- [ ] 2 tbsp oil
- [ ] &#x20;5cm (2in) cinnamon stick
- [ ] &#x20;5 green cardamom pods
- [ ] &#x20;4 cloves
- [ ] &#x20;8-12 curry leaves
- [ ] &#x20;5cm (2in) piece pandan leaf
- [ ] &#x20;5cm (2in) piece lemongrass
- [ ] &#x20;100g red onion (approx. 1 small onion), finely chopped
- [ ] &#x20;1 tbsp minced garlic
- [ ] &#x20;1 tbsp minced ginger
- [ ] &#x20;1 large piece goraka
- [ ] &#x20;3 tbsp Roasted Curry Powder (page 38)
- [ ] &#x20;3 green chilis halved lengthwise

## Instructions

1. Combine the pork with ½ tbsp chilli powder, the black pepper and ½ tsp salt. Cover and set aside for 1 hour to marinate.
2. Heat the oil in a deep heavy-based pan over a medium heat and add the cinnamon, cardamom and cloves. After 30 seconds, add the curry leaves, pandan leaf, lemongrass, onion, garlic and ginger. Cook for about 4 minutes until the onion turns slightly brown. Add the remaining ¾ tbsp chilli powder, remaining 1 tsp salt and the goraka and cook for a minute. Add a splash of water to get the charred bits off the pan and stir well. Add the pork and coat well in the spices, then pour in just enough water to cover the meat and bring to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook until the meat is tender, approximately 30 minutes.
3. Add the roasted curry powder and cook for a further 10 minutes. This is traditionally a dry curry but if you prefer a little bit of sauce, add some water in the final stage of cooking and adjust the seasoning accordingly. Stir in the green chillies and cook for a final 2 minutes, then serve straight away.
